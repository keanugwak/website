Priority 1 — Urgent

Clear the NAB credit card

At ~20% p.a. the $6,336 balance costs ~$105/month in interest alone — $1,260 burned per year. Redirect the $548/month pay-rise gain directly to extra NAB repayments. At $700/month extra you clear it in ~9 months.

Target: $0 by Jan 2027

Priority 2 — Urgent

Build a $5,000 emergency buffer

Zero visible savings means one unexpected expense forces more credit card debt. Auto-transfer $200/fortnight to a separate high-interest savings account. Target 3 months' essentials (~$15,000) long-term.

$200/fn → $5,000 in 25 weeks

Priority 3 — Near-term

Consolidate dual gym memberships

Anytime Fitness (~$92/month) AND Western Leisure (~$95/month) together cost $187/month — two separate memberships. Dropping one frees $95/month ($1,140/year) toward debt clearance or savings.

Potential saving: $95/month

Priority 4 — Near-term

Set a dining-out weekly cap

Dining runs $263–$317/month across frequent small purchases. Set a hard $200/month cap. Frequent $5–$30 charges (Sushi & Nori, Crumb, fast food) are hardest to track — a weekly limit works best for a family.

Target: save ~$100/month

Priority 5 — Medium-term

Review education costs annually

Heathdale ~$860/month and Adventurers ~$290/month are the second-largest cost after the mortgage. As kids get older, review whether all activities are essential. Reducing Adventurers frequency 20% saves ~$58/month.

$1,168/month — review each year

Maximise superannuation
